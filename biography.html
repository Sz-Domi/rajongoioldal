Biography

From formation to almost quitting
to mainstream success!

The formation

Tyler Joseph first met Nick Thomas in Ohio. And the two would remain friends throughout high school. While attending Ohio State University, Joseph met Chris Salih at a party. Salih consulted Joseph about starting a band. Joseph agreed to play music together and began sharing his ideas for new music. Just before their first performance, Joseph invited Thomas to join the unnamed band as a bassist. In 2009, their first album was conceptualized and recorded in the basement.

The start

the group released their debut album, Twenty One Pilots, on December 29, 2009, and began touring Ohio. During this time, the band participated in various "Battle of the Band" contests.
Salih left the band on May 8, 2011, to focus on work, and Thomas left the following month on June 3 to focus on schooling
Before departing from the project, Salih invited Josh Dun to take his place in the band.
Two years later they released Regional At Best According to cleveland.com, Joseph was contemplating giving up on music around the time of Regional at Best's release.

Gaining Traction

In April 2012, the band announced their signing to Atlantic Records subsidiary Fueled by Ramen
Their third album, Vessel, was released on January 8, 2013; It was their label debut. This was their first charting album.

breakthrough

On May 19, 2015, their biggest album to this day was released. Blurryface. On it's first week, it charted No. 1 album on the Billboard 200. stressed out became the group's best-selling song, having been certified 13 times Platinum, while also surpassing over 2.8 billion views on YouTube. On June 16, 2016, the band released the song Heathens as the first single from the Suicide Squad soundtrack

Mainstream

The duo released their fifth album, Trench, on October 5, 2018.
Trench debuted at No. 2 on the Billboard 200.
On April 9, 2020, the duo released the song Level of Concern, It references COVID-19 pandemic-induced anxiety, Joseph directed a portion of the song's proceeds to a charity for live music crews who could not be paid during the lockdowns.

The duo's sixth studio album, Scaled and Icy, was released on May 21, 2021. The album's name is an anagram, which spells out "Clancy is dead"

The present

On February 15, 2024, the cover art of Vessel, Blurryface, Trench, and Scaled and Icy were updated to be partially covered in red tape on streaming platforms. hinting at a forthcoming album.
On February 29, the duo's seventh studio album, Clancy, was announced for release on May 17
After posting a cryptic image to their social media platforms, it was confirmed on September 12, 2024 that the band would feature on the soundtrack for season 2 of the Netflix series Arcane, with the song The Line.
